  • Commonly used indicators in this portfolio:

    • Relative strength index (RSI): This indicator measures the momentum of the market by trying to determine overbought or oversold conditions on an asset. The RSI is best used as a valuable complement to other stock-picking tools.

    • RSI = 100 – 100/(1 + RS*)

    • Moving average convergence/divergence (MacD): This is a trading indicator created by Gerald Apple in the late 1970s It is supposed to reveal changes in the strength, direction, momentum and duration of a trend in a stock’s price. The MACD indicator (or “oscillator”) is a collection of three time series calculated from historical price data, most often the closing price.

    • ABC: One of the simplest yet effective patterns for trading is the “zigzag correction pattern” also known as “ABC”.  The analyst will be using the 15 min time period to determine a spike in the market on a trend which will eventually endure a correction and then a reformation back to the original trend direction.

    • Stochastic: An indicator that is always recommended for use in analysing the market, this indicator measures the fatigue of the market, and will virtually give you the exact timing on pullbacks. The indicator is based on “Theory of probability”.
