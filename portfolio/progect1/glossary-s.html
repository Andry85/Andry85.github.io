Glossary

SCALPER

Refers to a trade who takes very short term trades in an effort to ‘scalp’ small profits from high probability, short term trades. Trades can last from 1-5 minutes although they can go for longer periods such as 15 or 30 minutes.

SECONDARY MARKET

Another market that provides transactional liquidity for securities which may have been previously listed.

SECURITIES

Refers to financial instruments which can be traded. Can refer to stocks, commodities, stock indexes, currencies, bonds, T-Notes, etc.

SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE COMMISSION (SEC)

Federal Oversight Agency tasked with the security of all United States financial and securities markets, including regulation of licensing, etc.

SECURITIES INVESTOR PROTECTION CORPORATION (SIPC)

A special fund authorized by the US Congress to assist investors with the recovery of funds where the security of such has been compromised. Funds from the SIPC reserve are available to satisfy the remaining claims of each customer up to a maximum of $500,000. This figure includes a maximum of $100,000 on claims for cash.

SELL TO CLOSE

Order execution to close a long options contract position. Buy to open order is used to open a long options contract position.

SELL TO OPEN

Order execution to open a short options contract position. A buy to close is necessary to close out this position.

SEP IRA

Selling an options contract to initiate a position that will benefit from price decrease or depreciation. You would then execute a call or buy contract to close out the position.

SHORT POSITION

Selling an options contract to initiate a position that will benefit from price decrease or depreciation. You would then execute a call or buy contract to close out the position.

SHORT STOCK POSITION

Selling stocks or a stocks contract to initiate a position that will benefit from price decrease or depreciation. You would then execute a call or buy contract to close out the position.

SIDE

Refers to buyers or sellers – which represents both ‘sides’ in a transaction.

SIMPLIFIED EMPLOYEE PENSION (SEP) PLAN

Selling an options contract to initiate a position that will benefit from price decrease or depreciation. You would then execute a call or buy contract to close out the position.

SILVER XAG

Silver or Argento (from Vulgar Latin Plata, Argentum) is a chemical element with the symbol Ag and atomic number 47 (47 protons and 47 electrons). At room temperature, the solid is silver. In a flame test, silver takes took the color of lilac. Silver historically has been used as a liquid currency in most civilizations as much as gold.

Silver has a currency symbol for trading on the FOREX, this designation is XAG, the ISO 4217 which rounds to one troy ounce of .999% pure silver bullion. The price of silver varies more than that of gold. It is generally observed that the price of silver can augment variations on the rising and falling of the price of gold. The price of ingots varies depending on the market for an ounce of silver and its purity. Silver like gold is in fact the subject of a dual listing on the London market and the New York market.

SPOT PRICE

The price that is actually quoted for the commodity of an options contract which is also the same as cash commodity price.

SPREAD

The spread is always the difference between the ‘bid’ and the ‘ask.’ This can refer to currencies, options, and other securities. This is also an options limited reward strategy that combines options in an effort to profit from opposing moves in the price of the stock.

S&P 500 / STANDARD & POOR'S 500 INDEX

The index which lists 500 of the largest publicly traded US Companies. Considered the most accurate representation of the overall market.

STOP ORDER

An order given to your broker, or automated with your platform to purchase or sell a security when the price of that security reaches a pre-determined level at which you want to stop or close the trade either due to reaching profit or reaching an acceptable level of loss. When price reaches your objective the stop order then reverts to a market order and is executed at the best possible price.

STRADDLE

An options strategy where a call and a put are either both bought or both sold, executed with identical strike prices and identical expirations but the long straddle has unlimited profit and the short has limited profit and unlimited risk.

STRIKE PRICE

Also called the Exercise Price which is specified by the options contract – this is the price at which the holder of the options contract can purchase the underlying instrument or stock.

SUPPORT

The opposite of resistance in a market, asset or underlying security. Support represents the floor or the point where price bounces back up from support, from coming down. Resistance is when price bounces on the ceiling after going up, and bounces downward from resistance. One price bounce does not determine support, but repeated price action stopping on a specific price level, like 00 lends support. For example, if the GBP/JPY (Pound / Yen Currency Cross) has bounced at 173.00 then that may be a natural line of support or resistance. Support lines or resistance lines can reverse once they are pierced. For example, if price of the GBP/JPY comes down, and bounces 2 or 3 times at 173.00, then falls below to 172.50, 173.00 could now become resistance if price moves back up.

SWISS FRANC (CHF)

The Swiss Franc (CHF) is the official currency of Switzerland. Nicknamed the ‘Cheffie’, as of April, 2013, the Swiss Franc accounted for more than 5.2% of the daily foreign exchange turnover ranking 6th behind the AUD. The Swiss Bank, Switzerland UBS AG, accounts for 10.9% of the Daily Forex Volume. The Swiss Franc is also considered a ‘reserve’ currency. Once considered a safe haven currency, the Swiss Franc no longer holds this distinction.
