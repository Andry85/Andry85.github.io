TECHNICAL ANALYSIS important term

Technical analysis is the study of price action as reflected on charts of a market, asset class, or underlying instrument and the study of various indicators of derived classes (underlying) in order to anticipate the evolution, and subsequent direction of markets and the specific asset which you are trading.

This graphical extrapolation applies to all types of markets: indexes, equities, fixed income, commodities, options, futures, etc. It is not limited to equity markets; the same tools and methods that are mastered can then be applied to any (other) type of underlying asset when its price is determined by the meeting of supply and demand.

The main tool of the technical analyst is the graph that allows the visualization and analysis of an underlying asset. Regardless of the fundamentals, regardless of whatever may be occurring in the market now or in the future, the graphical chart reflects exactly what is happening at any specific moment in time.

Hence, successful trading ultimately boils down to your ability to ‘see’ – trading is a visual acuity, your ability to recognize repetitive chart patterns and to act, to execute instantly upon recognition of a trend reversal pattern, recognition of support, resistance, retraces, pull backs, etc.

The objective, the goal and purpose of trading, and therefore technical analysis is the ability to correctly forecast or recognize trends, and if possible, the signs of trend reversals. It involves identifying market conditions that give statistically identical, repetitive results; at least to a statistical probability of a minimum of 70%. 85% is preferable and attainable.

Technical analysis does not purport to be an exact science. It is closer to a human science as its object of study is directly focused on understanding the psychology of the market and how human nature does have waves, bio rhythms, repetitive patterns and behavior, for example, the Fibonacci retracement tool is based on absolute mathematical patterns in nature and thus, human nature.

  • Principles

    There are three fundamental axioms that governs and is the foundation of technical analysis:

    1. The market takes everything into account.

      This axiom is certainly the most important of all 3 because it justifies the fact that technical analysis is only interested in current, at the moment, market data and not to fundamentals such as economic statistics and business results. Indeed, if, at any moment, the price of an underlying asset immediately incorporates all available information on the markets, then there is no need to focus on the fundamentals as it is almost impossible to enjoy an announcement effect.

    2. Courses follow trends.

      It is the observation of historical price charts which gives the technical analyst the market trends. The study of a stock chart identifies phases, increases and decreases in phases over periods ranging from minutes to years.

      To explain this phenomenon is not just due to chance, technical analysis is based on the psychological analysis of crowds. Market, as a crowd, can be taken either in a dynamic optimism (or euphoria), or in a dynamic pessimism (or despair), and in a hesitation phase. This is reflected in a graph by periods of rising, falling or sideways or stagnant prices; times we will identify respectively as uptrend, as downtrend, and as range.

    3. History repeats.

      There is a popular, almost famous disclaimer on all financial corporate sites, including ours – that past performance isn’t indicative of the present or future – words to that effect. Here’s the truth – the future is determined by the past market and the same "mistakes" always repeat (formation of speculative bubbles and crashes).

Again, this idea stems from the psychological analysis of a crowd. Sociologists who are interested in crowds claim that they have identified specific behaviors having no link with a simple sum of individual behaviors. A crowd is driven by certain principles that manage to transcend the psychology of the individual component. All will have more influence on the part that each party will have in the least because of the phenomenon of imitation existing within any group.

The decision of an individual in a crowd is based on the idea that it is the decision that will persuade the other individuals in the crowd and not the normal perception of reality. This applies to markets that crash and speculative bubbles which are recurring; these are the best examples of the existence of the principle of imitation, which prohibits any lesson from the past to the crowd.

Laws or principals or repetitive patterns are to be noticed by technical analysts and then to attempt to take advantage of the recurring phenomena; techies know that there are no absolutes and that they usually employ several confirming scenarios before a decision, rather than take action on one technical factor.

As a trader, you should consider technical analysis as a practical approach to trading than as a perfect science and know that in order to execute winning trades, you will always have losing trades. No one trades at 100% no matter how experienced and skilled they may be.

The strength of technical analysis which can lead many to consider it as a science (social and not hard) is that it is produced and continually used by traders who have a daily and pragmatic view of the markets and continually behave in predictable patterns, proving the efficacy of this approach to the markets. A famous quotes with respect to technical analysis is that of George Lanes: "The trend is your friend" – this sentence sums up the advantages but also controversial technical analysis.

THEORETICAL VALUE

Black-Scholes is a mathematical formula for predicting fair theoretical value.

THETA

Greek letter for a change in an options contract’s value.

TICK / TICK CHART

On tick charts, this is the incremental price movement shown as a ‘tick’ – when price moves higher, this is an uptick, when price moves lower, this is a downtick.

TIME DECAY

Refers to the loss in value in an options contract as time continues to pass to expiration of the contract.

TIME SPREAD

By purchasing and selling options at the same strike price but different expiry times you get a time spread which is also another term for a calendar options spread strategy or a horizontal options spread strategy. An example of going long would be buying the September 45 Call and Selling the August 45 Call.

TIME VALUE

How much the current options exceeds the intrinsic value is the out of money price or at the money options price which is always extrinsic value or time value.

TOTAL MONEY MARKETS & CASH

Your total accounts except mutual fund amounts. Includes money market, margin, and cash.

TRADER

One who participates in the purchase and sale of securities in the pit, over the counter, online, offline, however the transactions take place. Usually refers to short term or one who only holds positions for limited periods of time. Sometimes referred to as an investor, the terms can sometimes have different meanings. An investor in real estate is not one who trades. A FOREX day trader may not be an investor, holding a cash account at a brokerage exclusively for profits gained from taking short term positions in a security. Also refers to the people who are on the floor or pits of exchanges.

TRAILING STOP

This is a risk / money management strategy or technique whereby you first place a stop loss before or immediately after you execute a trade (very advisable to activate stop losses before the execution of any trade or trading). Let’s say that the trade you executed starts to win, go in the direction favorable to your trade – up if you bought, down if you sold – a trailing stop automatically trails behind the trade. For example, let’s say your initial stop loss was 10. If your trade goes up 10, depending on the criteria set by you, the stop loss could move up or follow your trade. Your new stop loss would hypothetically be 0.

Trailing stops are used to protect profit as much as they’re designed to stop loss. A trailing stop loss can go in either direction, depending on the buy or sell order.

TRAILING (STOP) TRIGGER

This is the price or trigger that ‘stop’ activates.

TREASURY AUCTION

This is where the Treasury sells their newest issues of debt instruments such as bonds, notes, bills, etc.

TREASURY BILLS (T-BILLS)

Very short term debt instruments which are originated with the Treasury Department. From 3 months to one year.

TREASURY BOND (T-BOND)

This is a government debt instrument that is considered long term, can take up to 30 years to mature.

TREASURY NOTE (T-NOTE)

The T note is the mid or medium term debt instrument of the US Government. Time of maturation is normally 1-10 years.

TREND important term

Direction of a market, of an asset or a specific security such as a stock or a currency pair. The trend could be for 1 minute, for 30 minutes, 4 hours, 1 day, week, year, etc. The time frame is only important to the person who may be using the market direction to determine a trade or the best entry for a trade. For example, if I am watching the 1, min, 5 min, 30 min, and 4 hour chart of the EUR/USD and at the exact hour, on the hour, when all 4 charts are finishing one candle and another candle is starting, and all of them reflect the same color – for example, blue for going up, and they were all red for downward direction, and at the top of the hour they are all turning blue, you possible have a trend reversal.

Following the trend, understanding that “the trend is your friend” is one of the basic foundations of trading correctly and successfully. You must execute trades that are in the direction or trend of the market or you will get eaten like a shark. Or at least your account will.

TRENDLINE important term

A trendline, drawn correctly, shows the direction during a specific time period, of the price action of that security, asset or market. To draw a trendline correctly, you first determine visually in what direction the chart is showing price action.
