Glossary

H

HEAD AND SHOULDERS

This is a chart pattern, usually candlestick, which resembles first the left shoulder, then the head, then the right shoulder, the shoulders and head being peaks, the head being higher than the shoulders. The left shoulder is a trend peak, then there is profit taking and the trend re-emerges to a higher high, at which point it drops, and then rises again, except not a higher high – either it reaches the high of the left shoulder, or comes close, but this is typically a trend reversal pattern.

HEDGE

An investing or trading strategy with the objective of reducing or eliminating risk, protecting a position where you feel exposed. Maybe you are long on a stock, so you purchase a call option with a small amount of capital, but enough to offset any reversal in your position. It typically involves buying or selling options contracts or futures contracts that takes the opposing position against the initial trade or investment.

HEDGE FUND

Private, short term fund for investing and speculation in options, stocks, bonds, derivatives, currencies, and indices, in a high risk, high reward position, normally only high wealth individuals can participate, starting at one million USD and up. This is a private, unregulated investment fund for wealthy investors (minimum investments typically begin at US$1 million) specializing in high risk, short-term speculation on bonds, currencies, stock options and derivatives.

HEDGED POSITION

Normally, two positions which oppose each other in the currency market or foreign exchange, or options, used to protect one or other of the positions. Often used to hedge against loss on imports or exports and currency fluctuations on the FOREX.

HEDGING: A strategy designed to reduce investment risk using call options, put options, short-selling, or futures contracts. A hedge can help lock in profits. Its purpose is to reduce the volatility of a portfolio by reducing the risk of loss.

HIGH-YIELD BOND

A bond that has a high credit rating with Moody’s or S&P.

HORIZONTAL SPREAD

This is a spread strategy which is sometimes called a calendar spread whereby a trader will write or sell an option with the same strike price but different expiration as another option that is being purchased, taking advantage of implied volatilities. The option being purchased clearly has a lower implied volatility than the written option. If the opposite is done, this is a reverse calendar spread.

HARD CURRENCY

A measure of liquidity, referring to a well traded, easily converted currency. For example, the USD converted to EUR would be an easy transaction in virtually any country.

A different definition would be gold would be defined as hard currency and the U.S. Dollar would be ‘soft’ currency.
