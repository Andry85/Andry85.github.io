DAY ORDER

A day order is an order to execute that expires at the end of the trading day if not filled.

DAY TRADE

The purchase and sale of any position on the same trading day.

DAY TRADING

A style of trading where the investor is executing trades that open and close on the same day. Short term trading in a daily time frame usually using 30 minute or 4 hour charts in the foreign exchange or FOREX.

DEAL TICKET

The method of recording transactional data.

DEALER

A firm, an individual that performs duties of the principal and/or middleman, taking the opposing side or the middle of the trade with the objective of earning profit through the spread, fees, or commissions.

DEALING DESK / DEALING ROOM

A dealing desk refers to an institution’s trading room (or dealing room). The ‘dealing desk’ is where the corporate day traders sit, investing and trading the firm’s assets on a daily basis. The term is usually found when Forex Brokers are discussed. Some companies have no dealing desks where they state they are not trading against their investors.

DEBIT SPREAD (ALSO LIMIT/DEBIT)

An options strategy that is the opposite of a Credit Spread. It is the difference in worth of two options. Other examples of debit spreads are bull call spreads and bear put spreads.

DECAY (TIME DECAY)

Refers to the decreasing value of an option over time as it nears the final days, represented by the Greek letter theta.

DELIVERY (FUTURES)

Refers to transfer of commodities from sellers of futures contracts to buyers of futures contracts. Every exchange has set and detailed procedures for delivery of cash commodities.

DELIVERY (OPTIONS)

When a call writer or a put writer meets the financial obligations and requirements of an assigned options contract.

DELTA

The expected change in the value of an option. The delta of a put option will vary from -1.00 to 0. The delta of a call option will vary from 0 to 1.00.

DERIVATIVE (DERIVATIVE INSTRUMENT)

A derivative is a financial contract whose value is a direct correlation of the price fluctuations of a related underlying security instrument. The value is ‘derived’ from a stock, bond, commodity, a market index, or other type of security instrument. Options, futures, mortgage backed securities are examples of derivatives.

DEVALUATION

This is the deliberate decreasing or lowering of a currency’s value in relation to other currencies by a central bank to create a more competitive market for exports and other monetary policy manipulations.

DIAGONAL SPREAD

A position in which the trader buys and sells options with different strike prices and expirations. For example, a diagonal spread could be created by buying one July 75 call and selling one June 70 call.

DIAMONDS

A unit investment trust that holds 30 component stocks of the DJIA - Diamonds Trust Series which tracks the DJIA.

DOW JONES INDUSTRIAL AVERAGE (DJIA)

The representational index of the United States Stock Market which represents price action of 30 blue chip stocks.

DRAWDOWN

Drawdown refers to the decrease in value of an account as a direct result of trading losses. For example if you have a $50,000 account size and you trading during the day decreased the account down to $45,000, and then you went back up to $50,000 or even higher, you would have had a ‘drawdown’ of $5000, even though you never incurred any losses.
