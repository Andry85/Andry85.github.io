WAREHOUSE RECEIPT

A guarantee that a certain quality and quantity of a commodity exists and is held in a storage facility and is used as an ownership transfer document or instrument.

WARRANT

An Investment Trust or company can issue securities which give the holder purchase rights of shares at a designated future price. These are called warrants and they are also a tradable security pegged in value of course to the fluctuating share price. One of the features of a warrant is that a small drop in the price of the share price results in a large drop in the value of the warrants, and vice versa. Similar to options, the owner does not have to purchase the shares at any price as he has the right, not the purchase obligation.
