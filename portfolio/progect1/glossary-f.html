Glossary

FACE VALUE

Refers to the ‘face’ or $ value of a U.S. Treasury Note when it matures. Treasury Bills are sold or issued in the beginning with a discount and over time they mature to ‘face’ value.

FAIR VALUE

According to the value theorized by formulas when the market and the predicted value align.

FED

The U.S Federal Reserve.

FEDERAL FUNDS RATE (FED FUND RATE)

This is the overnight interest rate that banks charge for loans, controlled by Federal Reserves and Central Banks.

FEDERAL RESERVE (FED)

The Central Bank of the United States.

FEDERAL RESERVE BOARD (FRB)

7 presidential appointees who sit for 14 years, and loan banks establishing policy on currency, sets the discount.

FEDERAL RESERVE BANK

12 Banks, in designated cities which they control the activities of regional banks, monitoring and controlling commercial banks and savings and loans banks, acting as depositories, providing access to emergency real currency or actual cash.

FEDERAL RESERVE SYSTEM

The central banking system in the United States.

FILL

Execution.

FILL OR KILL

Instructions you would give your broker who is filling orders stating that you want it filled at your price parameters or the order should be killed or canceled.

FINRA

The Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (Formerly referred to as NASD). It is considered to be the largest non-governmental regulating authority which governs the way securities firms do business in the USA.

FLOOR BROKER

Refers to traders who are ‘in the pit’ or actually trading and executing investor orders there on the exchange floor.

FLOOR TRADER

Refers to a trader who is trading on behalf of his or her account acting as a market maker.

FOREIGN EXCHANGE
Introduction to the FOREX (The Foreign Exchange)

The Foreign Exchange, or more commonly referred to as the Forex (or the Currency Exchange, 4X or FX), is a global cash market where national currencies are paired against other national currencies and then traded. For example, the Euro is traded or paired against the US Dollar, as the euro exchange rate changes, the US dollar exchange rate changes.

  • Forex Trading Hours

    Forex trading starts non-stop from the Australian Open Sunday Evening at 22:00 – 07:00 GMT, Tokyo opens at 00:00 GMT, the London Open at 08:00 GMT to the New York Market open at 13:00 to the close on Friday afternoon at 22:00.

  • Largest, Most Liquid Financial Market

    The FOREX trades an approximate value of $4-$5 TRILLION (USD) every day, approximately 10 times more than ALL the financial markets in the world, combined; approximately 80 times more than these 3 stock markets – NYSE (New York Stock Exchange), NASDAQ, and AMEX - which only trade a combined $40 billion daily.

    This includes trading between central banks, commercial banks, Investment Banks, and large multi-national corporations. It also includes hedge fund managers, currency brokers, institutional and multinational corporations, governments, currency speculators, day traders and others.

    Historically, traders / investors which trade the FOREX markets have been central and commercial banks, Investment Banks, large multi-national corporations, institutional investors, and hedge funds managers. FOREX (4X; FX)

The most recognized abbreviation or acronym for The Foreign Exchange.

FORWARD (CASH) CONTRACT

Customized not standardized futures contract not traded on exchanges obligating a seller to deliver real or actual commodity sometime in the future or forward.

FREE RIDING

Purchasing securities or shares or some other type of financial instrument without actually having or needing the funds to cover the trade. This can typically occur if you are closing out a position before the funds have been settled.

FUND ASSETS

Sum total of current assets in whichever fund is being discussed.

FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS

This is the evaluation of a financial instrument or security using traditional financial data and accounting data as it relates to that instrument, i.e. – Price Earnings Ratios (P/E Ratio) revenue, etc. See Technical Analysis.

FUNGIBILITY

The capability to execute trades of the same financial or security instrument across various financial markets or commodity or stock exchanges or other marketplaces.

FUTURES CONTRACT

A futures contract is an agreement made on the trading floor obligating the seller to sell and the buyer to purchase a commodity or financial instrument and to accept delivery during a specific month in the future at terms which are agreed upon before execution.

FUTURES CASH

These are the required funds, which are segregated until the position is closed, after a futures position is filled.

FX

Another abbreviated form or acronym for The Foreign Exchange.
