ENKA Properties (ENKA) is one of the most active and respected real estate development and investment firms in New York. ENKA has developed, owned and managed more than 35 million square feet of commercial, residential and retail space in the U.S.

Founded in 1957 by President and CEO Larry Silverstein, SPI offers fully integrated real estate services and has a consistent track record of delivering quality projects on time and on budget as well as high returns on investments.

The firm specializes in complex mixed-use developments. Currently, Silverstein Properties has $10 billion worth of development activities underway, including rebuilding the commercial office space at the World Trade Center, one of the world's most complex mega-projects.

Silverstein Properties has developed, acquired and renovated some of most financially successful and high-profile properties, including the Ronald Reagan Building and International Trade Center, the largest office building in Washington, D.C.; Teleport, the world's first satellite communication center linked to a regional high-speed fiber optic network; 120 Wall Street, New York's only Non-Profit Association Center; and 7 World Trade Center, New York City's first LEED-certified (Gold) office building.
