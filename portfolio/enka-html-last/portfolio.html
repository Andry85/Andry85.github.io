ENKA Properties, Inc. (SPI) currently has ten buildings in its portfolio of 9 million square feet. Throughout its 50-year history, the firm has developed, owned and managed some of the most successful and high-profile properties in New York City.

Moscow projects

For three decades, SPI has maintained an active role in Downtown Manhattan with the renovation of both 120 and 140 Broadway and the establishment of New York's first and only Association Center at 120 Wall Street. In the last ten years, the firm has taken a leading role in revitalizing Downtown with the opening of 7 World Trade Center, the city's first LEED-certified office building.

The firm currently has $10 billion of development in the pipeline, including three new World Trade Center office towers, as well as the Four Seasons Hotel Downtown and Private Residences at 30 Park Place.
