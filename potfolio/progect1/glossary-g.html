Glossary

G

G

G Countries are leading industrial nations that meet usually on neutral grounds or where their summits take place. You have G-7, G-8, G-10, & G-20 meetings, and conferences of finance ministers, central bankers, and national leaders promoting international financial well-being.

G7

The G7 is the acronym for the 7 leading industrial countries. U.S.A., Germany, Japan, France, UK, Canada, Italy. Russia makes G8 although Vladimir Putin has created unrest and Russia was excluded from the last summit.

G8

The seven leading industrial countries, US, Germany, Japan, France, UK, Canada, Italy plus Russia.

G10

G7 plus Belgium, Netherlands and Sweden, a group associated with the IMF discussions. Switzerland is sometimes peripherally involved.

G20

This group includes the following countries: Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Japan, Mexico, Russia, Saudi Arabia, South Africa, South Korea, Turkey, the United Kingdom, the United States and the European Union. Attendance by the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank is normal. The G designation and this specific formal gathering of national financial representatives is created with the objective of promoting global financial stability, as opposed to financial and market turmoil.

GDP

Gross Domestic Product is the total annual production value of a country domestically, not internationally

GNP

Gross National Product – would be domestic production including international production or that from overseas investments and work from citizens working in different countries, but declaring that income.

GOING LONG

Opposite of going short. In a trade, according to any chart being used, and the chart patterns being used, every trade has 3 directions – up, down, sideways. Going long, purchase and hold is what a trader would do if he or she is inclined to an up direction on a specific asset and the purchase and hold. This could involve calls on options, purchases of stocks, commodities, currencies, etc., for the single purpose of buying for the ‘long term’ speculation and/or investment, not short term day trading.

GOING SHORT

Selling because you believe an asset or instrument will be losing value, so you go short by selling the currency, the commodity, or instrument even though you do not own any of the stock, cash commodity, etc.

GOLD STANDARD

In the Bretton Woods agreement, gold was pegged at $35 an ounce, and this was the gold standard. In 1971, this was discontinued and the U.S. Dollar (USD) then became a fiat currency. What actually occurred is that the USD then became a reserve currency, used to back other national currencies as opposed to gold being used. Switzerland used to have a law mandating a 40% reserve of gold to back the Swiss Franc.

GLOBEX

This is the platform of CME Group which provides 24 hour access to the global markets and the largest selection of options, futures and financial instruments in the world.

GOOD-UNTIL-CANCELLED (GTC)

This is an execution order that remains open until filled, cancelled or completed. Day orders expire at days end, these do not.

THE GREEKS

Analytical trading tools used to manage risk - Delta, Gamma, Rho, Theta and Vega.
