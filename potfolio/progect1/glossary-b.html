BACK-END LOAD

When mutual funds are sold, there is a deferred sales charge called a back-end load. Front end load is taken upon purchase as opposed to at the end when sold.

BACKSPREAD

A spread strategy where you have more long positions than short positions. This is a limited risk, unlimited profit strategy.

BALANCE OF TRADE

The net value of a country's international trades including all exports and its imports.

BANK RATE

The Central Bank Rate, used to lend money domestically.

BASE CURRENCY (VS. QUOTE CURRENCY)

Currency is traded in pairs or crosses – one is your ‘base’ currency, the first currency quoted in the currency cross or pair, and the other is the ‘quote’ currency - buying one currency while simultaneously selling the other. For example, the Euro is normally paired against multiple currencies such as the U.S. Dollar (or EUR/USD), the British Pound (sometimes referred to as the British Cable - EUR/GBP), or the Australian Dollar (EUR/ AUD).

The currency pair shows how much of the quote currency is needed to purchase one unit of the base currency. The ‘bid’ (buy price) represents how much of the quote currency is needed for you to get one unit of the base currency. Conversely, when you sell the currency pair, you sell the base currency and receive the quote currency. The ‘ask’ (sell price) for the currency pair is what you would receive in the quote currency for selling one unit of base currency.

BEAR SPREAD

A multiple options spread strategy that wins with a decline in the option or stock price.

BEAR CALL SPREAD

This strategy involves selling a call with a lower strike and buying a call with a higher strike. The highest profit is achieved when the stock trades at or below the lower strike.

BEAR PUT SPREAD

Spread which takes the purchase of a put with a higher strike price and the sale of a put with a lower strike price, achieving highest profit at or below the lower strike.

BEARISH

This term describes market sentiment of pessimists – those who expect the market to go down as opposed to up.

BEST ASK OR BEST OFFER

BEST BID

The highest security price quote or offer from all market makers.

BID

BID/ASK SPREAD

The difference between the bid and ask.

BID SIZE

The number of futures or options contracts bid at a certain price.

BRETTON WOODS AGREEMENT OF 1944

This agreement established the post war currency and foreign exchange market. It initially fixed gold at $35 an ounce, and fixed currency rates. In 1971 President Nixon created the floating exchange rate thereby overturning the agreement and replacing the fixed rates.
