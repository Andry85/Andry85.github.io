IMF – INTERNATIONAL MONETARY FUND

A fund started in 1946 to provide short term liquidity to help support countries with loan and payment issues on a global market.

IMPLIED VOLATILITY

Measures the expected range of price in the market of a currency future based on premiums.

IMPLIED RATES

The rate received when the difference is calculated between forward rates and spot rates.

INDICATIVE QUOTE

A non-committal price from a market-maker.

IMPLIED VOLATILITY

The implied or expected movement of price of a stock based on the current price of the stock option.

  • Index Types

    Indices (indexes) are typically classified (categorized) according to how the price of that index is determined. One category is price weighted indices such as the Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) and the AMEX Major Market Index.

    The price of each stock within the index is the only criterion upon which the value of the index is computed. Another different type is the market value weighted index, sometimes called a capitalization weighted index Here are a few examples of Market Value Weighted Indices / Capitalization Weighted Indices:

    • NASDAQ-100
    • NYSE Composite
    • FTSE-100
    • Hang Seng Index in Hong Kong
    • RTS Index
    • Russell 2000
    • S&P 500 - Now float-weighted

    An index which tracks a nation’s stock market performance, which naturally correlates investor sentiment is a national index, which are also the most quoted. For example, the American S&P 500, the British ‘footsie’ - FTSE 100; the Japanese Nikkei 224, etc. The FTSE Developed Asia Pacific Index and the FTSE Developed Europe Index are examples of what are labeled as ‘regional’ indexes.

INDEX OPTION

Similar to all options except its price and performance is related to an entire stock or market index rather than a single underlying instrument or stock.

INDICATED ANNUAL DIVIDEND

Usually this represents an annual amount based on the quarterly dividend to be paid to shareholders.

INDIVIDUAL ACCOUNT

When you open an account with Liberty Banc (or other brokerages) you are assigned an individual account as opposed to funds being mingled, co-mingled with other individual asset accounts.

INDIVIDUAL RETIREMENT ACCOUNT (IRA)

A retirement account designed to defer the payment of taxes. These are normally structured by banks, financial advisors, the company you work for, etc. For other types of IRAs, see Keogh plan, Simplified Employee Pension (SEP) plan, 401(k), Roth, or Rollover IRA.

INFLATION

When the price of consumer goods increases, it decreases the purchasing power of the general public; an economic state or condition due to disproportionate movement in price.

INITIAL MARGIN

The amount of collateral necessary to enter a position without having to commit the entire cash cost. This determines one’s trading limits. Also called “original margin” or “maintenance margin.

INITIAL MARGIN REQUIREMENT

See above – refers to the individual trade cash requirement.

INTER-BANK RATES

This is the quoted foreign exchange rates applied to deposits between interacting international and central banks. These rates from the foundation of the Interbank Market.

INTERMARKET SPREAD

The difference in the spreads of a commodity in two or more different markets, such as the CBOT quote on Corn as opposed to the market price in Kansas City.

INTERMEDIARY BANK

The bank used by your bank to conduct interbank activities on your behalf for example electronic funds transfers, wires, etc.

INTRINSIC VALUE

This is the true present value of an option or in-the-money. For example, with a $75 value of a stock, a 70 call trading at 5.25 has $5 of intrinsic value (75-70) and $1.25 of extrinsic or time value ($5.25-$4).

IRON BUTTERFLY

This is different from the butterfly because it this limited risk, limited reward spread strategy uses a combination of calls and puts. The Butterfly just uses all calls or all puts. The body is a long or short straddle, and the wings are long or short strangles.

ISE – INTERNATIONAL STOCK EXCHANGE.

Completely electronic International Stock Exchange.

ISO 26000

ISO 26000 offers guidance on socially responsible behavior and possible actions. The Seven Key Principles, advocated as the roots of socially responsible behavior, are

    1. Accountability
    2. Transparency
    3. Ethical behavior
    4. Respect for stakeholder* interests
    5. Respect for the rule of law
    6. Respect for international norms of behavior
    7. Respect for human rights

    The Seven Core Subjects, which every user of ISO 26000 should consider, are:

    1. Organizational governance
    2. Human rights
    3. Labor practices
    4. Environment
    5. Fair operating practices
    6. Consumer issues
    7. Community involvement and development
